Laser for PCOS-Related Hair Growth

Many of our clients come in for facial and body hair caused by PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome) or hormonal imbalance. Laser hair removal is safe and effective for hormonal hair, though it may require more sessions and periodic maintenance as new follicles activate over time.

Our dermatologist will discuss your medical history and set realistic expectations, and can coordinate with your endocrinologist if you are already on hormonal treatment.

Patch Test Protocol

Before your first full session, we perform a patch test on a small area to confirm the laser settings are safe for your skin. This is a small but important step — especially for medium to dark Indian skin tones — to prevent burns, pigment changes or unexpected reactions.

The patch test is complimentary and usually done during your consultation. You will only proceed to a full session once your skin has been assessed as safe to treat.

When Will I See Results?

Most clients begin to notice slower regrowth and softer hair after the first few sessions. Visible reduction builds gradually over the course because laser targets hair during its active growth phase — and that timeline varies by area, hair thickness and hormones. We review your response at every visit so expectations stay realistic and clear.

Risks, Side Effects & Suitability

Possible Side Effects

Laser Hair Removal is generally well tolerated, but you should be aware of possible short-term effects:

  • Mild redness and warmth in the treated area (typically resolves within a few hours)
  • Temporary mild swelling around hair follicles (perifollicular oedema)
  • Rarely: transient skin darkening or lightening, especially in darker skin tones
  • Very rarely: superficial blistering if aftercare instructions are not followed

Serious adverse events are uncommon. All procedures are performed under sterile conditions by trained medical staff under dermatologist supervision.

Who Should Avoid This Treatment

Laser Hair Removal is not recommended if you are:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Active skin infection, herpes outbreak or open wound in treatment area
  • Recent tan or sunburn (within 2 weeks)
  • Taking isotretinoin (Roaccutane) or other photosensitising medication in the last 6 months
  • History of keloid scarring
  • Under 16 years of age

Your dermatologist will review your full medical history during the free consultation before recommending treatment.
