What Is HIFEM & How Does It Work?

HIFEM stands for High-Intensity Focused Electromagnetic energy. The device causes supramaximal muscle contractions — far stronger than voluntary exercise. Each 30-minute session can simulate thousands of crunches or squats in the targeted area.

  • Strengthens muscle: Supramaximal contractions build and strengthen muscle fibres, increasing tone and definition.
  • Builds muscle: Intense contractions stimulate fibres to grow thicker and stronger (hypertrophy) and can create new fibres.
  • Dual effect: In one course, you build muscle and improve tone and definition in the same area.

You lie comfortably; a paddle-like applicator is strapped over the treatment zone while you remain fully clothed or in a treatment garment.

Risks, Side Effects & Suitability

Possible Side Effects

HIFEM Body Toning is generally well tolerated, but you should be aware of possible short-term effects:

  • Muscle soreness similar to intense exercise for 24-48 hours after session
  • Mild redness of the skin over the treatment area
  • Temporary muscle spasms during the session (part of the intended treatment)

Serious adverse events are uncommon. All procedures are performed under sterile conditions by trained medical staff under dermatologist supervision.

Who Should Avoid This Treatment

HIFEM Body Toning is not recommended if you are:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Metal implants, IUD, pacemaker, defibrillator or other electronic implants
  • Copper IUD in the abdominal treatment area
  • Cardiac conditions or history of arrhythmia
  • Recent surgery in the treatment area
  • Bleeding disorders, epilepsy or fever
  • Under 18 years of age

Your dermatologist will review your full medical history during the free consultation before recommending treatment.
