Risks, Side Effects & Suitability

Possible Side Effects

Hydra Facial is generally well tolerated, but you should be aware of possible short-term effects:

  • Very rare: mild redness or slight tightness right after the session
  • Occasional minor breakouts as impurities clear (short-lived)

Serious adverse events are uncommon. All procedures are performed under sterile conditions by trained medical staff under dermatologist supervision.

Who Should Avoid This Treatment

Hydra Facial is not recommended if you are:

  • Pregnant clients: mild version only, avoid active-acid steps
  • Active skin infection, cold sore, severe rosacea flare or sunburn
  • Fresh dermal fillers or Botox in the treatment area (within 2 weeks)
  • Taking isotretinoin (Roaccutane) in the last 6 months

Your dermatologist will review your full medical history during the free consultation before recommending treatment.
