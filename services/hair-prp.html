What Is Hair PRP?

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) is a natural treatment that uses growth factors from your own blood to stimulate hair follicles, slow hair loss and encourage regrowth. A small blood sample is drawn, processed to concentrate the platelets, and gently injected into the scalp under dermatologist supervision.

Hair PRP is ideal for:

  • Women experiencing hair thinning or diffuse hair fall
  • Early to moderate androgenetic (pattern) hair loss
  • Post-pregnancy or stress-related hair shedding
  • Anyone who prefers a drug-free, natural regrowth option

Our dermatologist will assess your scalp, hair pattern and medical history to confirm PRP is the right treatment for you.

What to Expect During a PRP Session

1

Consult & Blood Draw

Dermatologist reviews your scalp and hair concerns. A small blood sample is drawn from your arm.

2

Platelet Concentration

Blood is spun in a centrifuge to separate and concentrate the platelet-rich plasma.

3

Scalp Injection

PRP is gently injected into the thinning areas of the scalp using ultra-fine needles. A topical anaesthetic is applied to minimise discomfort.

4

Back to Your Day

No downtime. You may resume normal activities immediately, with light aftercare instructions.

Results & How Many Sessions You Need

1–2

Sessions: Reduced Hair Fall

A standard course is 4–6 sessions spaced about 4 weeks apart, followed by maintenance every 4–6 months.

Risks, Side Effects & Suitability

Possible Side Effects

Hair PRP is generally well tolerated, but you should be aware of possible short-term effects:

  • Mild scalp tenderness, redness or swelling at injection sites (typically resolves within 24–48 hours)
  • Small bruises at injection points
  • Temporary headache or mild scalp itch
  • Very rarely: infection or allergic reaction to topical anaesthetic

Serious adverse events are uncommon. All procedures are performed under sterile conditions by trained medical staff under dermatologist supervision.

Who Should Avoid PRP

Hair PRP is not recommended if you are:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Diagnosed with blood disorders, low platelet count, or clotting conditions
  • On blood-thinning medications (e.g. warfarin, aspirin therapy)
  • Currently on chemotherapy or immunosuppressants
  • Living with active scalp infection or skin condition
  • Under 18 years of age

Your dermatologist will review your full medical history during the free consultation before recommending treatment.
