Multi-Colour Tattoo Removal

Our Q-Switched Nd:YAG system handles multi-colour tattoos — not just black. Different laser wavelengths target different ink colours:

  • Black & dark blues: 1064 nm wavelength — typically the fastest to clear
  • Reds, oranges, yellows: 532 nm wavelength
  • Greens, sky blues, teals: may need extra sessions and a combination of wavelengths

During your consultation the dermatologist maps your tattoo's ink colours and depth and gives you a realistic session estimate.

Recovery & Downtime

Laser tattoo removal has minimal downtime. Immediately after your session you may see mild redness, slight swelling and a light frosting effect over the ink — all of which settle within a few hours to a day.

You can return to work and normal activities the same day. We ask you to avoid sun exposure, hot water, sauna, swimming and heavy workouts for 48–72 hours to protect the treated area.

The treated tattoo will gradually fade over the following weeks as your body naturally clears the fragmented ink through the lymphatic system.

Our 5-Step Tattoo Removal Process

A structured, dermatologist-supervised approach designed for safety and results on Indian skin.

1

Consultation

Our dermatologist evaluates your tattoo's size, colour, depth, and skin type to create a personalised treatment plan with realistic expectations.

The Q-switched laser delivers targeted pulses that break down tattoo pigment into tiny particles, which your body naturally eliminates over time.

You receive detailed aftercare instructions including wound care, sun protection, and follow-up scheduling. Sessions are spaced 6-8 weeks apart for optimal healing.

Risks, Side Effects & Suitability

Possible Side Effects

Laser Tattoo Removal is generally well tolerated, but you should be aware of possible short-term effects:

  • Temporary redness, mild swelling and pinpoint bleeding immediately after the session
  • Blistering, scabbing or crusting for 1-2 weeks (part of normal healing)
  • Rarely: temporary pigment changes (lighter or darker patches)
  • Rare: partial permanent pigment change, especially with darker skin or coloured tattoos

Serious adverse events are uncommon. All procedures are performed under sterile conditions by trained medical staff under dermatologist supervision.

Who Should Avoid This Treatment

Laser Tattoo Removal is not recommended if you are:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Recent tan or sunburn on the tattooed area
  • Active skin infection, herpes outbreak or open wound in the area
  • Taking isotretinoin (Roaccutane) in the last 6 months
  • History of keloid scarring
  • Photosensitive conditions

Your dermatologist will review your full medical history during the free consultation before recommending treatment.
