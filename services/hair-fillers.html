What Are Hair Fillers?

Hair fillers are dermatologist-administered scalp treatments that deliver a blend of hyaluronic acid, peptides, amino acids and vitamins into the mid-dermal layer of the scalp. Unlike Hair PRP — which uses growth factors from your own blood — hair fillers use a lab-prepared bio-restorative blend designed to hydrate the scalp environment and support healthier hair growth.

Hair fillers may suit you if you have:

  • Early-stage hair thinning or diffuse hair fall
  • A dry, dehydrated or irritated scalp affecting hair quality
  • Fine, brittle or dull-feeling hair that lacks body
  • Post-pregnancy or stress-related shedding you want to address
  • Been considering PRP but prefer a non-blood-based option

Our dermatologist will assess your scalp, hair pattern and medical history to confirm hair fillers are the right treatment — and whether combining with PRP or topicals is worth considering.

Risks, Side Effects & Suitability

Possible Side Effects

Hair fillers are generally well tolerated, but you should be aware of possible short-term effects:

  • Mild scalp tenderness, redness or swelling at injection sites (typically resolves within 24–48 hours)
  • Small bruises at injection points
  • Temporary itching or mild scalp sensitivity
  • Very rarely: infection or allergic reaction to the filler blend or topical anaesthetic

Serious adverse events are uncommon. All procedures are performed under sterile conditions by trained medical staff under dermatologist supervision.

Who Should Avoid Hair Fillers

Hair fillers are not recommended if you are:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Diagnosed with an active scalp infection or skin condition
  • Living with an autoimmune skin disorder affecting the scalp
  • Known allergic to hyaluronic acid or peptide-based products
  • Currently on chemotherapy or immunosuppressants
  • Under 18 years of age

Your dermatologist will review your full medical history during the free consultation before recommending treatment.
