Who Is It For?

Chemical peels are ideal for anyone looking to address these common skin concerns:

Acne & Acne Scars

If you struggle with recurring breakouts or post-acne pigmentation, targeted peels help clear active acne and fade scars over time.

Pigmentation & Melasma

Dark patches, sun spots, and hormonal pigmentation respond well to carefully selected peels that inhibit excess melanin production.

Dull & Uneven Skin

If your complexion looks tired, patchy, or lacks radiance, a chemical peel revives your natural glow by removing the dull outer layer.

Fine Lines & Aging

Early signs of aging such as fine lines, loss of elasticity, and rough texture are effectively treated with medium-depth peels.

Oily & Congested Skin

Excess oil, clogged pores, and blackheads are addressed with salicylic-based peels that deep-cleanse without over-drying the skin.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a chemical peel?

A chemical peel is a dermatologist-administered treatment where a medical-grade acid solution is applied to the skin to exfoliate the outermost layers. This stimulates cell turnover, revealing fresher, smoother, and more even-toned skin underneath.

Is a chemical peel safe for Indian skin?

Yes, when performed by a qualified dermatologist, chemical peels are safe for Indian skin. Our dermatologist selects the peel type, concentration, and duration specifically suited to your Fitzpatrick skin type to minimise any risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation.

How many sessions are needed for visible results?

Most clients notice a visible glow after the first session. For concerns like pigmentation, acne scars, or fine lines, a course of 4 to 6 sessions spaced 2 to 4 weeks apart is recommended for optimal, lasting results.

Is the chemical peel treatment painful?

You may feel a mild tingling or warm sensation during the peel application, which subsides quickly once the peel is neutralised. The procedure is well tolerated and does not require anaesthesia.

What is the downtime after a chemical peel?

Downtime depends on the peel depth. Superficial peels have minimal downtime with mild flaking for 1 to 2 days. Medium-depth peels may involve light peeling for 3 to 5 days. Your dermatologist will advise you based on the peel selected for your skin.

Can chemical peels help with acne and acne scars?

Yes, chemical peels are highly effective for active acne and post-acne marks. Salicylic acid peels help unclog pores and control breakouts, while glycolic and TCA peels promote collagen remodelling to fade acne scars over multiple sessions.
