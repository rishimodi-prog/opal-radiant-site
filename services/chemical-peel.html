If you struggle with recurring breakouts or post-acne pigmentation, targeted peels help clear active acne and fade scars over time.

Dark patches, sun spots, and hormonal pigmentation respond well to carefully selected peels that inhibit excess melanin production.

If your complexion looks tired, patchy, or lacks radiance, a chemical peel revives your natural glow by removing the dull outer layer.

Risks, Side Effects & Suitability

Possible Side Effects

Chemical Peel is generally well tolerated, but you should be aware of possible short-term effects:

  • Redness, tingling and warmth during and immediately after application
  • Visible peeling, flaking or dryness for 3-7 days
  • Temporary sun sensitivity for up to 4 weeks
  • Rarely: temporary pigment changes (darker or lighter patches), especially in deeper skin tones

Serious adverse events are uncommon. All procedures are performed under sterile conditions by trained medical staff under dermatologist supervision.

Who Should Avoid This Treatment

Chemical Peel is not recommended if you are:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding (for stronger peels; some mild peels may be safe under supervision)
  • Active skin infection, herpes outbreak, eczema flare or sunburn
  • Taking isotretinoin (Roaccutane) in the last 6 months
  • History of keloid scarring or poor wound healing
  • Recent tanning, laser or other chemical peel (within 2 weeks)
  • Allergy to peel ingredients

Your dermatologist will review your full medical history during the free consultation before recommending treatment.
