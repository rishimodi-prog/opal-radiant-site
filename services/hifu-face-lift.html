Who Is an Ideal Candidate for HIFU?

HIFU Face & Neck Lift is best suited for women in their 30s to 60s who are experiencing mild to moderate skin laxity and want a non-surgical alternative to a traditional facelift. You may be an ideal candidate if you have:

  • Sagging cheeks or jowls that have lost their youthful volume
  • Loss of jawline definition or a softening facial contour
  • Loose, crepey skin on the neck or under the chin
  • Fine lines and wrinkles that make you look older than you feel
  • A desire for visible lifting and tightening without surgery, injections, or downtime

Frequently Asked Questions

HIFU stands for High-Intensity Focused Ultrasound. It is a non-invasive cosmetic procedure that uses focused ultrasound energy to target deep layers of the skin, stimulating collagen production and resulting in tighter, lifted skin on the face and neck — without surgery or downtime.

Most clients experience mild discomfort and a tingling sensation during the treatment as the ultrasound energy is delivered to the deeper skin layers. The sensation is generally well-tolerated, and no anaesthesia is required. Any discomfort subsides immediately after the session.

Risks, Side Effects & Suitability

Possible Side Effects

HIFU Face Lift is generally well tolerated, but you should be aware of possible short-term effects:

  • Mild redness or flushing immediately after treatment (usually resolves within hours)
  • Temporary tingling, tenderness or mild swelling in treated areas
  • Transient numbness in some spots (typically resolves within days to weeks)
  • Rarely: small bruises or temporary muscle weakness in the treated area

Serious adverse events are uncommon. All procedures are performed under sterile conditions by trained medical staff under dermatologist supervision.

Who Should Avoid This Treatment

HIFU Face Lift is not recommended if you are:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Metallic implants, permanent facial fillers or thread lifts in the target area
  • Pacemakers or other electrical implants
  • Active skin infection, cystic acne, or open wounds in the treatment area
  • Bell's palsy or other facial nerve conditions
  • Bleeding disorders or use of anticoagulant medication

Your dermatologist will review your full medical history during the free consultation before recommending treatment.
