What to Expect During a Fat-Freeze Session

1. Consult & Plan

We review your goals, confirm you're a suitable candidate and mark the target area, then plan how many applicator cycles you'll need.

2. Set-Up & Comfort

A gel pad is placed and the cooling applicator is positioned over the area; you'll feel intense cold and strong suction for the first few minutes, which quickly numbs.

3. Controlled Cooling

The applicator cools the area to a precise temperature for the full cycle — about 35–60 minutes — while you relax, read, work or nap.

4. Massage & Back to Routine

The applicator is removed and the area is briefly massaged to boost fat breakdown. There's no downtime — you can return to work or errands immediately.

Is It Right for You?

Are You an Ideal Candidate for Fat Freeze?

Fat freeze works best for women close to their target weight who still struggle with stubborn pockets of fat that don't respond to diet or exercise. It's a body-contouring treatment — not a weight-loss shortcut.

You may be a good candidate if…

  • You have noticeable fat bulges in specific areas
  • You are within a healthy weight range
  • You want a non-surgical alternative to liposuction
  • You are looking for long-lasting fat reduction in specific areas
  • You prefer zero downtime after treatment

Risks, Side Effects & Suitability

Possible Side Effects

Fat Freeze (Cryolipolysis) is generally well tolerated, but you should be aware of possible short-term effects:

  • Temporary numbness and tingling in the treated area (up to 2 weeks)
  • Redness, mild swelling or bruising at the applicator site
  • Firmness or sensitivity in the treated area for a few days
  • Rarely: pinching sensation during the first few minutes of cooling

Serious adverse events are uncommon. All procedures are performed under sterile conditions by trained medical staff under dermatologist supervision.

Who Should Avoid This Treatment

Fat Freeze (Cryolipolysis) is not recommended if you are:

  • Pregnant or breastfeeding
  • Cryoglobulinemia, paroxysmal cold haemoglobinuria or cold urticaria (cold sensitivity conditions)
  • Hernia in or near the treatment area
  • Significant loose or damaged skin, or active skin conditions in the area
  • Recent surgery or medical implants in the target area
  • Under 18 years of age

Your dermatologist will review your full medical history during the free consultation before recommending treatment.
