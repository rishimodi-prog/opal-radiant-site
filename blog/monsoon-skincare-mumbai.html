Monsoon Skincare in Mumbai: A Dermatologist's Guide

By Dr. Punit Sakhare, MD Dermatologist · 2026-07-02 · 5 min read

Mumbai monsoon is beautiful. It's also the worst season for skin. Humidity, sweat, changing weather, and reduced sun exposure combine to create unique challenges. Here's what I tell my patients every June.

The Monsoon Skin Problems

Increased breakouts (humid air + sweat + oily skin) — a cross-sectional study of acne patients in tropical Delhi found that both temperature and humidity correlated significantly with seasonal flares, with aggravation most commonly reported in the summer and rainy months (Narang et al., 2018). Also common: fungal infections (in body folds), scalp itchiness/dandruff, dull skin (less sun = less natural vitamin D), and paradoxically, dry patches (humidity + AC combo).

Prioritise Cleansing

Wash your face 2–3 times a day with a gentle cleanser — more if you're sweating a lot. Salicylic acid-based cleansers help oily, acne-prone skin; in professional-strength peel form, salicylic acid was among the more effective chemical-peel options for acne in a recent systematic review of procedural therapies (Pour Mohammad et al., 2025), though at far higher concentrations than a daily cleanser. Don't over-scrub; that damages the skin barrier and worsens breakouts.

Fight the Fungus

Body folds (underboob, groin, underarms) are prime real estate for fungal infections during monsoon. Hot, humid conditions are a documented risk factor for yeast and fungal folliculitis outbreaks, as seen in a reported case series among residents of a non-air-conditioned, humid facility (Jalalat et al., 2014). Keep them dry, use antifungal dusting powders after showers, and change out of wet clothes immediately. See a dermatologist for stubborn fungal infections.

Don't Skip Sunscreen

Common mistake. Even when it's cloudy, UV rays still reach the skin — measurements of ambient UV in shaded and unshaded settings found that increasing cloud cover actually reduces the protective benefit of shade, because more radiation arrives as scattered, diffuse light rather than direct sun (Moise & Aynsley, 1999). Use a lightweight, non-comedogenic SPF 50+. Gel formulations work well for oily skin.

Treatments to Prioritise in Monsoon

Monsoon is actually a great time to start laser hair removal — sun exposure is naturally lower. Also excellent for pigmentation treatments (Carbon Facial, peels) because your sun exposure is reduced.

Treatments to Avoid or Delay

Aggressive medium/deep chemical peels — delayed healing in humidity. Fresh tattoos or body treatments where healing needs a dry environment.

Home Care Adjustments

Switch from creamy to gel moisturisers. Use lightweight, water-based skincare. Add a clay mask 1–2x a week for pore refinement. Keep hands off your face — humidity + touching = breakouts.

Umbrella and Rain Gear Matter

Rain water (especially urban Mumbai rain water) isn't clean. If your face gets wet, wipe with a clean cloth and rinse with clean water at home. Rain-soaked hair can drip onto shoulders and trigger back/chest acne.

Post-Monsoon Recovery

Once monsoon ends (usually late September), plan a Hydra Facial or gentle brightening peel to restore glow. Many patients notice their skin is duller after monsoon — this is normal and quickly corrected.
