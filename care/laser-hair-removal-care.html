Laser Hair Removal — Pre & Post-Care Guide

Following these pre-care and post-care instructions makes your laser hair removal session more comfortable, more effective, and safer for your skin. Your practitioner will review these with you at your consultation and answer any questions.

See Laser Hair Removal details ›

Please tell us before booking if any of the following apply to you:

  • You are pregnant or breastfeeding
  • You are taking isotretinoin or other photosensitising medication
  • You have a personal or family history of keloid scarring
  • You have recently tanned or self-tanned the treatment area

These don’t always rule out treatment, but they change how we plan your session. See our full Safety & Protocol guide for details, and if you’re pregnant, read Laser Hair Removal and Pregnancy Safety.

Before Your Session

In the days leading up to your appointment:

  • 2–4 weeks before: Stop waxing, threading, plucking or bleaching the treatment area. Laser targets the hair root, and removing it beforehand leaves nothing for the laser to work on.
  • 2 weeks before: Avoid direct sun exposure, tanning beds and self-tanners. Tanned skin absorbs more laser energy and increases the risk of burns or pigment change.
  • 1 week before: Stop using retinoids, glycolic acid, salicylic acid or other exfoliating products on the treatment area.
  • 24 hours before: Shave the treatment area cleanly with a fresh razor. This is important — do not skip this step. Long hair on the surface reduces effectiveness and can cause discomfort.
  • On the day: Come with clean skin — no makeup, deodorant, lotion, oil or perfume on the treatment area. Wear loose, comfortable clothing.

After Your Session

After your session:

  • First 24 hours: Mild redness, warmth or slight swelling around the hair follicles is normal and usually settles within a few hours. Apply a cool compress if the area feels warm.
  • First 48 hours: Avoid hot showers, saunas, steam rooms, swimming pools and intense exercise. Heat can irritate treated skin.
  • First 2 weeks: Apply a broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen daily on any exposed treated area. Sun exposure is the biggest cause of complications after laser.
  • First 2 weeks: Do not wax, thread, pluck or use hair-removal cream. You may shave between sessions if needed.
  • 1–3 weeks after: Treated hair will appear to grow, then shed on its own. This is normal — it is the follicle expelling dead hair, not new regrowth.
  • Between sessions: Keep the area moisturised. Use gentle, fragrance-free cleansers on the treated area for a few days.
  • If you notice small bumps or ingrown hairs: This can happen as hair sheds between sessions. Gentle exfoliation once skin has settled usually helps — see our evidence-based guide on preventing ingrown hairs after laser.

When to Call Us

Please contact your clinic on +91 77030 37070 if you experience any of the following after your Laser Hair Removal session:

  • Blistering, prolonged pain, or skin that feels hot to the touch beyond 24 hours
  • Any signs of infection — increasing redness, warmth, pus, or fever
  • Unusual dark or light patches that appear in the treated area

These are precautionary — serious problems are uncommon, but early contact is always the safer choice.
