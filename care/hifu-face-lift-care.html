HIFU Face Lift — Pre & Post-Care Guide

HIFU stimulates collagen deep in the skin, and results build up over 2–3 months. Following these instructions keeps your session comfortable and helps you get the most from your treatment.

See HIFU Face Lift details ›

Before Your Session

Before your HIFU appointment:

  • 2 weeks before: Do not have chemical peels, microneedling, laser treatments, or Botox on the areas being treated.
  • 1 week before: Avoid dermal fillers in the treatment area if you can — space them at least 2 weeks apart from HIFU.
  • 3–5 days before: Stop retinoids and exfoliating acids on the treatment area. Keep skin hydrated.
  • 48 hours before: Where medically safe to do so, minimise alcohol and blood-thinning medication (aspirin, ibuprofen, high-dose fish oil) to reduce bruising risk. Do not stop any prescribed medicine without asking your doctor.
  • On the day: Arrive with clean skin — no makeup, sunscreen or moisturiser on the treatment area. Eat a normal meal beforehand.

After Your Session

After your HIFU session:

  • Immediately: Mild redness or flushing is normal and usually fades within a few hours. Some clients feel a warm, tender or tingling sensation.
  • First 24 hours: The skin may feel slightly bruised or tender, especially along the jawline. This is a good sign — it means the treatment reached the intended depth.
  • First 3 days: Avoid saunas, steam rooms, hot yoga, intense workouts and very hot showers.
  • First 1–2 weeks: Apply broad-spectrum SPF 30+ daily. Keep skin well moisturised. Gentle cleansing only.
  • First 2 weeks: Do not have facials, peels, microneedling or other laser treatments on the area.
  • 2–12 weeks: This is when collagen remodels. You may notice gradual tightening, better contour and improved skin quality. Some clients see initial lift immediately, but the main results build slowly.

When to Call Us

Please contact your clinic on +91 77030 37070 if you experience any of the following after your HIFU Face Lift session:

  • Persistent numbness, tingling or muscle weakness beyond 2 weeks
  • Severe swelling or bruising that keeps worsening after day 3
  • Any signs of skin damage — blisters, open sores, unusual discolouration

These are precautionary — serious problems are uncommon, but early contact is always the safer choice.
