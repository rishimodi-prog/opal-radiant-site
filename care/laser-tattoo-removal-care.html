Laser Tattoo Removal — Pre & Post-Care Guide

Tattoo removal heals more like a laser wound than most aesthetic procedures. Careful aftercare means better fading, less scarring risk and safer sessions.

See Laser Tattoo Removal details ›

Before Your Session

Before your session:

  • 4 weeks before: Avoid sun exposure and tanning of the tattooed area. Tanned skin cannot be treated safely.
  • 2 weeks before: Where medically safe, stop non-essential blood-thinners (aspirin, ibuprofen, high-dose fish oil). Do not stop prescribed medication without checking with your doctor.
  • 1 week before: Keep the area well moisturised. No new lotions, self-tanners or fragranced products on the tattoo.
  • On the day: Come with clean, dry skin over the tattoo. No makeup, sunscreen or moisturiser on it. Wear loose clothing over the area.

After Your Session

After your session:

  • Immediately: The tattoo will look "frosted" (white-grey) briefly, then red and swollen. Some pinpoint bleeding is normal. We'll apply a soothing dressing.
  • First 24 hours: Keep the area covered as we've dressed it. Apply cool compresses over the dressing if needed (never ice directly). Take paracetamol if you need it — avoid ibuprofen for 24 hours.
  • Days 2–14: Blisters, scabs, crusting or minor bruising are normal parts of healing. Do not pick, scratch or peel scabs — this is what causes scarring.
  • First 2 weeks: Gentle cleansing only. Pat dry. Apply the aftercare ointment we've given you (or a simple healing ointment) 2–3 times daily. Keep the area out of direct water for the first 24 hours.
  • First 4 weeks: No swimming pools, hot tubs, saunas or vigorous exercise until the area is fully healed. Cover the area from the sun completely — a bandage or clothing is better than sunscreen while it heals.
  • Between sessions: Sessions are 6–8 weeks apart to give the body time to clear fragmented ink. Broad-spectrum SPF 50 daily on the area between sessions.

When to Call Us

Please contact your clinic on +91 77030 37070 if you experience any of the following after your Laser Tattoo Removal session:

  • Spreading redness, warmth, pus or fever — possible infection
  • Deep or unusually large blistering that does not settle
  • Any bleeding that does not stop with gentle pressure

These are precautionary — serious problems are uncommon, but early contact is always the safer choice.

Common Questions

Most tattoos need 6–12 sessions, depending on ink type, colour, depth, age and skin tone. Your dermatologist will give you a realistic estimate at your consultation.

When aftercare is followed and sessions are spaced properly, scarring risk is low. The single biggest cause of scars in laser tattoo removal is picking scabs — please don't.

Every OPAL Radiant treatment is dermatologist-supervised. If anything looks or feels off after your session, please call us before trying home remedies.
